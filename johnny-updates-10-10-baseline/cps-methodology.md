# Clearwater Performance Selling (CPS)

## Summary
CPS is Clearwater's defined sales methodology. It removes subjectivity from deal management through standardized process stages, objective scoring (P2V2C2), and structured tools (DAP, Approach Document). The core principle: there is a sales opportunity only if the pain of power is great enough to change from status quo. We win by co-creating a solution that supports the client's vision with agreed-upon value, while maintaining control of the process.

## Sales Process Roadmap — 5 Stages

### 1. DISCOVER
**Goal:** Identify a Champion with a compelling reason and willingness to change.

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 1A: Pre-qualify prospect | Research the company, identify if there's a fit |
| 1B: Identify potential Champion | Find someone willing to meet |
| 1C: Share Industry Trends Storyboard | Bring value, demonstrate expertise, get client talking |
| 1D: Champion shares their situation | Listen — look for evidence of compelling reason to change |

**Verifiable Outcome:** Potential Champion willing to change.

**Key guidance:** With few exceptions, we should NOT be talking about ourselves. We're looking for evidence of a compelling reason to change. Prospects don't buy until they've made the conscious decision to change their current business processes.

### 2. QUALIFY
**Goal:** Both you and the client truly understand the identified pain and have validated willingness to change. PES buys into the DAP.

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 2A: Validate Current State & Pain | Confirm compelling reason to change with Champion |
| 2B: Set expectations for process | Explain what's needed to get to a proposal |
| 2C: Gain access to power (ES) | CRITICAL — ask Champion for access to decision makers early |
| 2D: Define buying process & Prepare DAP | Understand client's desired timeline, work backward |

**Verifiable Outcome:** PES bought into Dual Action Plan (DAP).

**Key guidance:** CRITICAL ACTION = gaining access to power. The earlier we force the issue, the easier the remaining stages. The DAP should be introduced here — reviewed and agreed upon with the client, not just emailed.

### 3. PROVE
**Goal:** Client and Clearwater clearly align on the custom-tailored solution. ES verbally agrees to the business proposal.

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 3A: Determine Proof Criteria, Co-create | Co-creation meeting — collaborative, not selling. Talk success metrics with ES. |
| 3B: Document Approach (AD) | Capture Current State → Future State journey in the Approach Document |
| 3C: Validation Meeting | Present AD to client — NEVER just send it. Validate solution meets proof criteria. |
| 3D: Email AD with Pricing | Only after validation meeting confirms alignment |

**Verifiable Outcome:** ES verbally agreed to business proposal.

**Key guidance:** CRITICAL ACTION = Co-creation. This is where we show breadth of expertise and create a winning AD aligned to the client's "shining city on the hill." ADs must be PRESENTED, never just sent. ADs must be approved by Sales VP/Baxter/Dan/SC before presenting to client.

### 4. NEGOTIATE
**Goal:** Navigate the client's contracting process while holding them accountable to the DAP.

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 4A: Revise DAP with detailed steps to close | Align DAP with Contracting/Legal timeline |
| 4B: Present MSA/SOW | Formal contract presentation |
| 4C: Exchange red-lines & Negotiate | Vendor questionnaires, redlines, live negotiation |
| 4D: Client committed to signature date | Firm date agreed |

**Verifiable Outcome:** Negotiation with Legal & Procurement complete.

**Key guidance:** CRITICAL ACTION = understand ALL steps of contracting (who reviews, who signs, vendor questionnaires, etc.). Don't ever give without getting — negotiate to benefit both sides. Get ahead of this stage during Prove.

### 5. CLOSE
**Goal:** Contracts signed, warm hand-off to delivery.

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 5A: Both parties agree on terms | Final terms negotiated |
| 5VO: Contracts signed | Deal won |

**Verifiable Outcome:** Contracts signed.

**Key guidance:** Know who signs. Check if signer has planned time off. Launch DocuSign. Warm hand-off to Customer Success team.

## P2V2C2 Scoring Rubric (0-5 each, Total /30)

### PAIN — "What pain is driving this opportunity?"
Pain impacts the business and is felt by the Champion and Executive Sponsor.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | No knowledge of pain |
| 1 | PC has shared their situation |
| 2 | PC has admitted a pain |
| 3 | PC has indicated the pain of the PES |
| 4 | PES has admitted a pain |
| 5 | ES has agreed the pain is large enough to change |

**SF Description field:** Describe the Pain from either or both perspectives (Champion and/or ES).

### POWER — "Are we working with someone in power?"
| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | No idea who is power |
| 1 | Have an indication of a PES |
| 2 | Champion has agreed to take us to the PES |
| 3 | Have met with the PES |
| 4 | ES has agreed to the DAP |
| 5 | Steps of the DAP are on schedule |

**SF Description field:** Describe whether we are stuck at the Champion. Does ES need board approval?

### VISION — "Can we co-create a vision?"
| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | No idea of needs |
| 1 | We have begun to define needs |
| 2 | We have mapped our capabilities to their needs |
| 3 | They have agreed to proof criteria |
| 4 | ES has agreed to Vision of the improved future state |
| 5 | ES is painting the vision for others in the org |

**SF Description field:** Describe the ES's Vision of the "shining city on the hill."

### VALUE — "Does our vision have value?"
| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Benefits of our solution are unknown |
| 1 | Believe their situation is causing Pain |
| 2 | Have defined the cost of their Pain |
| 3 | Believe our solution can address their Pain |
| 4 | ES has shared benefits of our solution with DM |
| 5 | DM has agreed to financial terms |

**SF Description field:** Progress notes on Value/benefits Clearwater's solution will deliver.

### CHANGE — "Is there a commitment to change?"
| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | No one committed to Change |
| 1 | PC is open to change |
| 2 | PC has confirmed the PES will meet with us |
| 3 | PES has agreed they need to Change |
| 4 | ES has chosen Clearwater to lead Change |
| 5 | ES has convinced DM they must Change |

**SF Description field:** Status of their acknowledgement of necessity to change.

### CONTROL — "Who has control?"
| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Buying process is unknown |
| 1 | C has described the process as C knows it |
| 2 | Have met with a PES |
| 3 | Pain is admitted and a vision created for our ES |
| 4 | ES has agreed to the DAP |
| 5 | DAP is complete |

**SF Description field:** Progress notes on our progress toward Control.

### P2V2C2 Key Principles
- P2V2C2 is **always in motion** — scores can go up OR down
- P2V2C2 is a **snapshot** of deal strength at a point in time
- Must be evaluated on **every Opportunity update**
- Supporting descriptions in Salesforce must align with the rubric definitions above
- P2V2C2 facilitates critical thinking and enables accurate forecasting

## Key Client Roles

| Role | Definition | Key Traits |
|------|-----------|------------|
| **Champion (C)** | Internal advocate | Wants you to win. Provides access to power. Sells on your behalf when you're not around. |
| **Potential Executive Sponsor (PES)** | Not yet committed to us | Can do everything ES does but hasn't chosen Clearwater yet. |
| **Executive Sponsor (ES)** | Committed decision influencer | Buys into DAP. Can find the money or has the budget. Provides access to additional power. ES can support only ONE company. |
| **Ultimate Decision Maker (UDM)** | Final authority | Has final say but likely defers to ES. |
| **Economic Buyer** | Financial approver | Approves the price and financial terms of the investment. |
| **Victim** | Affected but passive | Feels the pain but not driving change. |
| **Adversary** | Actively opposed | Prefers competitor or status quo. |

## Dual Action Plan (DAP)

**What:** A project plan working backward from the client's desired start date. Each line item has a date, owner (Clearwater or Client), and status.

**When to introduce:** First or second call. Set the expectation early so it doesn't appear as a sales tactic later.

**Keys to success:**
- Be confident introducing it — it helps the client meet THEIR objectives
- Don't SEND the initial DAP — present it, get feedback, agree to steps together
- Set cadence to review with client; reset when there's slippage
- Always update as milestones complete; send to all stakeholders
- Always tie back to their objectives
- Never frame as part of our sales process — it's about the client's pain and timing
- Once MSA/SOW goes to legal/procurement, get a DAP with them too

**Date projection rule:** If DAP dates are not explicitly agreed upon, project logical future dates based on a standard successful sales cadence working backward from the client's desired start date. Do not leave dates blank — mark projected dates as [PROJECTED]. Anchor date = first discovery call unless otherwise stated.

### Current State / Future State Structure (mirrors SF Deal Highlights field)
Always break into three sub-categories:
- **ORGANIZATION:** Org structure, ownership, governance, relevant context
- **ACTIVITIES:** What they're currently doing (or not doing) re: security, compliance, risk
- **TECHNOLOGY:** EHR, cloud environment, endpoints, key systems

### Key Stakeholder Success Criteria
Track what success looks like from each key stakeholder's perspective:
- **TO THE BOARD:** What the board needs to see/hear to approve
- **[STAKEHOLDER NAME/ROLE]:** What this person personally needs to consider it a success

**Example DAP entries:**
| Activity | Due Date | Responsible | Status |
|----------|----------|-------------|--------|
| Review capabilities & program options | 4/19 | Clearwater / Client | complete |
| Co-Create session — Objectives, Scope, Vision | 4/23 | Clearwater / Client | complete |
| Present draft Approach for Strategic Program | 4/27 | Clearwater | complete |
| Go/No Go decision | 5/8 | Client | open |
| Submit for budget approval | 5/15 | Client | open |
| Budget approval | TBD | Client | open |
| Send SOW | TBD | Clearwater | open |
| Call to review redlines | TBD | Both | open |
| Execute contract | TBD | Both | open |
| Initial Engagement Meeting / Project Start | TBD | Both | open |

## Approach Document (AD)

**What:** Captures the client's Current State and the plan to get to their Future State, developed together during the Co-Create session.

**Why:** Demonstrates professionalism, commitment to client success, differentiates from competition. Most importantly, fosters better outcomes.

### Required Elements (items marked * are non-negotiable):
1. Industry Trends
2. Executive Summary
3. *Current and Desired Future State
4. *Success Criteria
5. The Challenge
6. *The Approach
7. Investment Summary
8. Why Clients Choose Clearwater
9. *Next Steps (DAP)

### AD Rules:
- ADs must be PRESENTED, never sent without a formal review
- ADs must be approved by Sales VP/Baxter/Dan/SC before presenting
- Submit final draft to approver 48hrs before meeting
- An AD cannot be completed without a Co-create meeting
- You must have an AD to request an SOW
- Upload to proper OneDrive location associated with Opportunity

## Opportunity Types — Inbound vs Outbound

90% of buyers are NOT actively looking. When the buyer calls us, they're already in Prove stage. But for us to gain Control, we must take them backward through Discover and Qualify. This is difficult but essential.
